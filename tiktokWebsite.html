What is TikTok?

TikTok is a social media app that allows users to create and share short-form videos, typically between 15 and 60 seconds long. The app was created by the Chinese tech company ByteDance and launched globally in 2018. Since then, it has become one of the most popular social media platforms in the world, particularly among younger generations. One of the key features of TikTok is its algorithm, which uses machine learning to analyze user behavior and preferences, and to suggest content that is likely to be of interest to them. This has made the app particularly popular for discovering new music and artists, as well as for discovering viral trends and challenges.

TikTok Scandal!

TikTok gained immense popularity in India with millions of users creating and sharing short videos on the platform. It became one of the most downloaded apps in the country. Concerns started to emerge regarding the type of content being circulated on TikTok. There were reports of inappropriate and explicit content, including instances of child pornography and cyberbullying. In April 2019, the Madras High Court in India issued a ban on TikTok following a public interest litigation (PIL) claiming that the app encouraged the spread of inappropriate and pornographic content. The court expressed concerns about the safety of children using the platform. As a result of the court order, TikTok was temporarily removed from app stores in India. However, TikTok's parent company, ByteDance, appealed the decision, and the ban was lifted after about a week when the court revised its order. The Government of India took notice of the concerns surrounding TikTok and initiated a formal investigation into the app's content moderation practices. The government expressed concerns about the potential misuse of user data and national security implications. In June 2020, the Indian government decided to ban TikTok and several other Chinese-owned apps citing concerns over data security, privacy, and their potential impact on India's sovereignty. This move came against the backdrop of escalating tensions between India and China following a border clash. The Indian government issued an official order to ban TikTok and other Chinese apps, directing app stores to remove them from their platforms. This led to the removal of TikTok from app stores, making it unavailable for download in India. TikTok's ban prompted legal challenges by the app's parent company, ByteDance, and other content creators. The legal battles are still ongoing, with appeals and hearings taking place in Indian courts.
