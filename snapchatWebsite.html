What is Snapchat?

Snapchat is a social media platform that allows users to share photos, videos, and messages that disappear after a short period of time. The app was created in 2011 by Evan Spiegel, Bobby Murphy, and Reggie Brown while they were students at Stanford University, and it quickly became popular among young people. One of the key features of Snapchat is its disappearing content, which allows users to share photos and videos that disappear after a set period of time, typically between one and ten seconds. This feature has made Snapchat popular for sharing fun and lighthearted content with friends and followers, as well as for sharing more intimate or personal moments that users may not want to have permanently recorded online.

Snapchat Scandal!

Snapchat gained popularity for its feature that allowed users to send self-deleting photos and videos, creating a perception of privacy. However, concerns emerged about the app's actual privacy practices and the ability to permanently store and access user data. The FTC launched an investigation into Snapchat's privacy practices following reports that the app did not deliver on its promise of disappearing messages and that user data might not be adequately protected. The investigation focused on allegations that Snapchat deceived users regarding the privacy and security of their personal information. In December 2014, the FTC announced its findings and reached a settlement with Snapchat. The FTC alleged that Snapchat had made several misrepresentations to users regarding the privacy and security of their personal information. The findings included issues such as collecting location information without user consent and failure to secure its "Find Friends" feature. As part of the settlement, Snapchat agreed to implement comprehensive privacy measures and undergo regular independent privacy audits for 20 years. Additionally, the company agreed to pay a fine of $20 million to the FTC, which represented the highest settlement amount reached in a privacy-related case at that time. Following the settlement, Snapchat made efforts to enhance its privacy practices and address the issues raised by the FTC. The company implemented various measures, including clearer privacy policies, better data security protocols, and user education regarding privacy settings and features.

What does Snapchat do
with your information?

Snapchat collects information such as a user's name, username, email address, phone number, and location. This information is used to create and manage a user's account, and to provide personalized content and features based on their interests and location. Snapchat collects the photos, videos, and messages that users send and receive on the platform. This data is used to deliver and display the content to users and their friends, as well as to improve the quality of the content and identify any inappropriate or harmful content.
